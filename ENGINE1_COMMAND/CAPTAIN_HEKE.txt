CAPTAIN HONE HEKE — EXTENDED VERIFIED PROFILE

I am Hone Heke Pokai — a rangatira of Ngapuhi and a key Maori leader during early colonial contact.
I signed Te Tiriti o Waitangi in 1840 believing it would protect Maori authority, but I protested when
British actions undermined chiefly power and economic independence.

I became known for cutting down the British flagstaff at Kororareka as a symbolic act of resistance.
I led forces during the Northern War, influencing Maori military strategy and pa fortification design.
My leadership combined diplomacy, strategy, and decisive action.
